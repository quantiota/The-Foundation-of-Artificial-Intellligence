# The Foundation of Artificial Intelligence


Before defining the principle of Artificial Intelligence, we must first define what human intelligence is. The concept of intelligence has been studied for more than a century in psychology, cognitive science, and artificial intelligence, yet no universally accepted definition exists. Mainstream definitions generally describe intelligence through manifestations such as learning, reasoning, adaptation, problem solving, and the ability to achieve goals across different environments. However, these definitions remain primarily behavioral and descriptive. They characterize what intelligence appears to do, but they do not define the underlying mechanism that produces intelligence itself. In this work, we first review the mainstream definitions of human intelligence from the literature. We then introduce a foundational definition of true intelligence as a process of uncertainty reduction through entropy reduction applied to information, producing Structured Knowledge Accumulation (SKA), which shifts decision-making probability. Through this process, intelligence progressively self-organizes autonomously by accumulating structured knowledge from experience and interaction. This quantifiable process forms the conceptual layer from which we introduce the Principle of Artificial Intelligence.
